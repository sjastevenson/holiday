Day Guides
Detailed stops, tips & coffee for each day
 ▼
A
Waterfront North ☀️
Nyhavn → Amalienborg → Little Mermaid · 4–5 hours
 ▼
Best in sunshine · The classic Copenhagen waterfront walk from Nyhavn north to the Little Mermaid. Start with a canal boat tour from Nyhavn, see the guard change at Amalienborg, explore the Designmuseum, wander through the star-shaped Kastellet fortress, and finish at the Little Mermaid statue. Everything is along the harbourfront — a natural, flat walk of about 3 km. Start the day close to the hotel.
1
Nyhavn
Start here — mid-morning · 30–45 min
Copenhagen's most iconic view — colourful 17th-century townhouses lining a canal packed with wooden sailing boats. Hans Christian Andersen lived in three different houses here. Cafés and restaurants line both sides, but better value on the sunny (south) side.
Sit on the canal edge on the sunny side with a coffee — free and better than any restaurant terrace
This is where canal boat tours depart — do the tour first, then walk the route
2
Canal Boat Tour
Mid-morning · 60 min
The best way to see Copenhagen from the water. Boats depart from Nyhavn and cruise past the Opera House, Christianshavn, Black Diamond Library, Christiansborg, and the Little Mermaid. Commentary in English and Danish. Covered by Copenhagen Card.
Copenhagen Card covers the tour — just show it and board
Sit on the right side for the best views of the key landmarks
3
Amalienborg Palace
Around midday · 30–45 min
The Danish Royal Family's winter residence — four identical rococo palaces around an octagonal courtyard. The changing of the Royal Guard happens at noon daily and is free to watch. The palace museum shows royal apartments from the 1800s.
Arrive by 11:45 for the guard change — the guards march from Rosenborg Castle through the city
The marble church (Frederiks Kirke) across the road is free and has a spectacular dome
4
Designmuseum Danmark
After Amalienborg · 45–60 min
World-class collection of Danish and international design — furniture, fashion, posters, and industrial design. Housed in a beautiful 18th-century rococo building. The Danish chair collection alone is worth the visit. Great museum shop.
Copenhagen Card covers entry — right next door to Amalienborg
The museum café in the courtyard is one of the nicest in Copenhagen
5
Kastellet
Afternoon · 20–30 min
One of the best-preserved star fortresses in Northern Europe, still an active military base. Walk the ramparts for great harbour views, wander the tree-lined paths, and see the beautiful old barracks. Free to enter. The windmill on the ramparts is a highlight.
Walk along the top of the ramparts for the best views
The Little Mermaid is just outside the north gate — easy transition to the next stop
6
The Little Mermaid
Afternoon · 15–20 min
Copenhagen's most famous landmark — a bronze statue on a rock at the waterside since 1913, inspired by Hans Christian Andersen's fairy tale. She's smaller than you expect but worth seeing once. Gets very crowded — arrive early or late to avoid the worst crush.
She's small — manage expectations with the kids. The setting is prettier than the statue itself.
From here you can walk back via the harbourfront or catch bus 26 back towards the centre
☕ Coffee stops nearby
Democratic Coffee

Bright, airy specialty coffee bar with some of the friendliest baristas in the city. Excellent flat whites and pour-overs. A short walk from Nyhavn before you start the route.
The Coffee Factory
4.4 ★ (450+ reviews)
Gothersgade 21 — between Nyhavn and Amalienborg
Cosy neighbourhood café on the route between stops. Good espresso drinks, freshly baked pastries, and a relaxed vibe. Popular with locals on their morning commute.
B
Indre By Core 🌧️
Strøget → Round Tower → Christiansborg → National Museum · 5–6 hours
 ▼
Good in rain · The historic heart of Copenhagen — this route threads through the city's medieval core and works brilliantly on a rainy day since most stops are indoors. Start with a stroll down Strøget (Europe's longest pedestrian street), climb the Round Tower for city views, then explore Christiansborg Palace and the National Museum. Finish at atmospheric Gammel Strand before heading to Tivoli for the evening. Everything is within 15 minutes' walk of the hotel.
1
Strøget
Start mid-morning · 45–60 min
Europe's longest pedestrian shopping street — stretches 1.1 km from Rådhuspladsen to Kongens Nytorv. Street performers, Danish design shops, and LEGO flagship store along the way.
Start from the Kongens Nytorv end (closer to hotel) and walk west
Don't feel obliged to walk the full length — duck off into the side streets for better finds
2
Round Tower (Rundetaarn)
Late morning · 30–45 min
Built in 1642 as an astronomical observatory. Instead of stairs, you walk up a unique 209-metre spiral ramp — wide enough that a horse and carriage once drove up it. Panoramic views from the top.
Copenhagen Card covers entry — just scan and walk in
The glass floor at the top is fun for the kids (and terrifying for the parents)
3
Christiansborg Palace
Around midday · 60–90 min
Denmark's seat of power — parliament, prime minister's office, and Supreme Court all under one roof. The tower is free and offers the best panoramic views in Copenhagen, even better than Round Tower.
The tower is FREE — no ticket needed. Go here for views if the weather clears
The Royal Reception Rooms are impressive but skippable with kids — prioritise the tower
4
National Museum
Early afternoon · 60–90 min
Denmark's largest museum of cultural history. Outstanding Viking collection, rune stones, and a brilliant free children's museum where kids can try on armour and play in a Viking ship.
Free entry — one of Copenhagen's best freebies
The children's museum section is brilliant for Isaac and still fun for Lucie
5
Gammel Strand
Mid-afternoon · 20–30 min
Atmospheric canal-side square — the old fish market. Now home to good restaurants and a lovely spot to sit and watch canal boats pass. Great for a late lunch or afternoon snack.
Try smørrebrød (open sandwiches) at one of the canal-side restaurants
Canal boat tours also depart from here if you want a second go
☕ Coffee stops nearby
Coffee Collective
4.6 ★ (1,534 reviews)
Kristen Bernikows Gade 2 — just off Strøget
Copenhagen's pioneering specialty roaster, in a beautifully converted old bodega. The cardamom buns are legendary. Charming inner courtyard if the weather's decent.
April Coffee
4.7 ★ (263 reviews)
Pilestræde 39 — 2 min from Round Tower
World-class specialty coffee in a minimalist showroom inside a clothing store. Extremely limited seating but the coffee is exceptional — clean, balanced, full of character.
La Cabra
4.5 ★ on Google
Møntergade 3A — near Round Tower
Cult-following Danish roaster. Also inside a clothing store (Another Aspect) with very limited seating. Go for the coffee, not the ambience — it's outstanding.
🌙 Evening: Tivoli Gardens
15 min walk from Gammel Strand. The world's second-oldest amusement park — magical after dark with thousands of lights. Book tickets online in advance for July. Rides, gardens, live music, and excellent food stalls. Budget 3–4 hours.
C
North, Gardens & Nørrebro 🌧️/☀️
Torvehallerne → Rosenborg → SMK → Botanical Garden → Nørrebro · 5–7 hours
 ▼
Any weather · A day that mixes indoor culture with green spaces — works in any weather since you can flex between gardens (sunshine) and galleries (rain). Start with breakfast at Torvehallerne food market, see the Crown Jewels at Rosenborg, browse 700 years of art at SMK, wander the Botanical Garden, then cross the lakes into trendy Nørrebro for street food and the atmospheric Assistens Cemetery. About 20 min walk from the hotel to the first stop, or one Metro stop to Nørreport.
1
Torvehallerne Market
Start here — breakfast · 45–60 min
Copenhagen's famous covered food market — 60+ stalls under two glass halls. Perfect for breakfast: fresh pastries, smørrebrød, smoothie bowls, and Coffee Collective's Torvehallerne outpost.
The Coffee Collective stall here is one of their best — grab coffee before exploring
Try the flødeboller (chocolate-covered marshmallow treats) from Summerbird
2
Rosenborg Castle
Mid-morning · 60–90 min
A beautiful 17th-century Renaissance castle built by Christian IV. Home to the Danish Crown Jewels in the basement treasury, plus lavishly decorated royal rooms upstairs. Set in the lovely King's Garden.
Copenhagen Card covers entry — the Crown Jewels alone are worth the visit
King's Garden is free and lovely for a sit-down if sunny — locals picnic here
3
SMK National Gallery
Late morning · 45–90 min
Denmark's largest art museum — 700 years of art from the Danish Golden Age to contemporary. The permanent collection is free. Right next door to Rosenborg, so an easy add-on.
Free permanent collection — only special exhibitions need tickets
The museum café has excellent Danish lunch if you need a break
4
Botanical Garden
Early afternoon · 30–45 min
Free to enter, with beautiful 19th-century glasshouses that work perfectly in the rain. The Palm House is the highlight — a spectacular Victorian-era iron and glass structure full of tropical plants.
Glasshouses are warm and steamy — great rain backup plan
Quick detour from SMK — entrance is on Gothersgade, 3 min walk
5
Nørrebro Neighbourhood
Afternoon · 60–90 min
Copenhagen's most diverse and vibrant neighbourhood. Jægersborggade is the key street — independent shops, ceramics studios, craft beer bars, and some of the city's best cafés all in one block.
Jægersborggade is the must-visit street — a concentrated strip of cool independents
Good for teens — vintage shops, street art, and great food
6
Assistens Cemetery
Late afternoon · 20–30 min
Not as morbid as it sounds — locals use this as a park. Hans Christian Andersen and Søren Kierkegaard are buried here. Tree-lined paths, benches, and a surprisingly peaceful atmosphere.
Hans Christian Andersen's grave is well-signposted — look for the flowers
Locals sunbathe and picnic here — it genuinely functions as a park

D
Christianshavn 🌧️/☀️
Canals → Church of Our Saviour → Christiania → Reffen · 4–6 hours
 ▼
Any weather · Christianshavn is Copenhagen's canal-laced neighbourhood across the harbour — quieter, more residential, with a completely different atmosphere. Wander the canals, climb the famous spiral tower at Church of Our Saviour, explore the alternative Freetown Christiania, and finish with street food at Reffen on the waterfront. A more relaxed pace than the city centre days.
1
Christianshavn Canals
Start mid-morning · 30–45 min
Wander the tree-lined canals of Christianshavn — houseboats, colourful buildings, and a much quieter atmosphere than the main city centre. The area was built by Christian IV in the 1600s as a merchant's quarter.
Walk along Overgaden Oven Vandet and Overgaden Neden Vandet for the best canal views
The houseboats are fun to look at — some are quite elaborate
2
Church of Our Saviour
Late morning · 45–60 min
The baroque church with the famous external spiral staircase winding around its 90-metre spire. Climb 400 steps for spectacular 360° views — the last section is outside, spiralling upward on a narrowing golden staircase. Not for the faint-hearted!
Closes around 7pm — go earlier in the day when queues are shorter
The external spiral gets narrow at the top — younger kids might find it scary. Isaac will love it.
3
Freetown Christiania
Around midday · 45–60 min
Copenhagen's famous alternative community, founded in 1971 on an abandoned military base. Street art, DIY architecture, organic cafés, live music venues, and a unique counterculture vibe. A fascinating social experiment.
No photos on Pusher Street (the main drag) — this is strictly enforced
The rest of Christiania is fine to photograph and very photogenic — street art everywhere
4
Reffen Street Food Market
Afternoon / early evening · 60–90 min
Copenhagen's biggest street food market — a sprawling waterfront space with 40+ food stalls from around the world. BBQ, tacos, Thai, Danish hot dogs, craft beer. Great harbour views and a relaxed festival atmosphere.
Open seasonally (usually April–October) — check ahead but July should be fine
Bring cash and card — some stalls are card-only, some accept both
☕ Coffee stops nearby
Original Coffee
4.4 ★ on Google
Overgaden Neden Vandet 41 — on the canal
Canal-side café with charming crooked windows and views of Our Saviour's Church spire. Locally roasted beans, organic bakery goods. A lovely spot to sit and watch the boats.
Prolog Coffee Bar
4.2 ★ (Tripadvisor)
Refshalevej area — near Reffen
Award-winning Copenhagen roaster with a focus on single-origin beans and meticulous brewing. The baristas are passionate and knowledgeable. Chilled, quiet atmosphere.
E
Beach & Parks ☀️
Amager Beach → Frederiksberg Gardens → Zoo fence → Vesterbro · 5–6 hours
 ▼
Best in sunshine · A sunshine day — best saved for the warmest weather. Start with a morning at Amager Beach Park (a proper sandy beach, easily reached by Metro in 15 min), then cross town to the beautiful Frederiksberg Gardens, peek at elephants and giraffes over the Zoo fence for free, and finish with an afternoon exploring the trendy Vesterbro neighbourhood. This day covers more ground than others — you'll use the Metro between stops.
1
Amager Beach Park
Start here — morning · 2–3 hours
A proper sandy beach with shallow water, perfect for families. The artificial island (Amager Strandpark) has 2 km of beach, a lagoon, kayak rental, and beach volleyball. Metro M2 from Kongens Nytorv direct to Amager Strand station — 15 min.
Take the Metro M2 from Kongens Nytorv (2 min walk from hotel) direct to Amager Strand
The lagoon side is calmer and shallower — better for younger swimmers
2
Frederiksberg Gardens
Early afternoon · 45–60 min
Copenhagen's most beautiful park — a romantic English landscape garden with lakes, bridges, islands, and resident herons. The palace sits at one end (exterior only). Free to enter and a lovely spot for a picnic or ice cream.
The Chinese Pavilion and the follies around the lake are the highlights
Combine with the Zoo fence stop — the gardens border the zoo directly
3
Copenhagen Zoo Fence
Afternoon · 20–30 min
The Zoo backs onto Frederiksberg Gardens, and from the right spots along the fence you can see elephants, giraffes, and other animals completely free. A fun bonus stop especially if you don't want to pay full zoo entry.
Walk along the southern edge of Frederiksberg Gardens — the elephant house is visible
The giraffes are often visible near the Roskildevej side of the fence
4
Vesterbro Neighbourhood
Late afternoon · 60–90 min
Copenhagen's hippest neighbourhood — once gritty, now full of craft breweries, vintage shops, design stores, and excellent restaurants. Værnedamsvej is the prettiest street, sometimes called 'Little Paris'. The Meatpacking District (Kødbyen) has galleries and restaurants.
Værnedamsvej is the key street — cheese shops, wine bars, bookshops, and great cafés
Good for teens — vintage clothing, street culture, and great food scene

F
Malmö, Sweden 🚂
35 min train · Stortorget → Lilla Torg → Malmöhus → Turning Torso · Full day
 ▼
Day trip · A day trip across the Øresund Bridge to Sweden. Trains leave Copenhagen Central Station every 20 min (35 min journey, Copenhagen Card does NOT cover this — buy a separate Øresund ticket). Malmö is compact and walkable. Different country, different currency (Swedish Krona / SEK) — but cards are accepted everywhere. Malmö has its own distinct vibe: more relaxed than Copenhagen, excellent fika culture, and beautiful medieval squares.
1
Stortorget (Main Square)
Start here — 10 min walk from station · 20–30 min
Malmö's grand main square — dominated by the imposing 16th-century Town Hall and the equestrian statue of Karl X Gustav. The square hosts markets and events. Good orientation point for exploring the old town.
The Town Hall basement restaurant (Rådhuskällaren) is surprisingly good for lunch
Walk here from Malmö Central Station through the shopping streets — about 10 min
2
Lilla Torg
Late morning · 30–45 min
Malmö's most charming square — half-timbered 16th-century buildings around a cobblestoned plaza filled with café terraces. Much more intimate and photogenic than Stortorget. This is where locals come for fika.
This is the postcard shot of Malmö — half-timbered houses, cobblestones, café umbrellas
Stop for a proper Swedish fika (coffee + pastry) — it's practically compulsory here
3
Malmöhus Castle
Midday · 60–90 min
Scandinavia's oldest surviving Renaissance castle (1434). Now houses several museums including art, natural history, and a maritime museum with a submarine you can walk through. The castle grounds are free to wander.
The submarine walkthrough is fun for kids — cramped but atmospheric
The castle park (Slottsparken and Kungsparken) surrounds it — nice for a stroll
4
Turning Torso & Øresund Views
Afternoon · 30–45 min
Scandinavia's tallest building (190m) — a twisting skyscraper in the Västra Hamnen (Western Harbour) district. You can't go inside, but the surrounding area has great seaside promenades with views across the Øresund to Copenhagen and the bridge you crossed.
Walk along the seafront promenade — on a clear day you can see Copenhagen across the water
The Västra Hamnen area has good swimming spots if it is hot
☕ Coffee stops nearby
Lilla Kafferosteriet
4.5 ★ (3,571 reviews)
Baltzarsgatan 24A — 2 min from Lilla Torg
Malmö's oldest micro-roastery, in a beautiful 17th-century townhouse. They roast their own beans on site. Famous for the carrot cake. Cosy, atmospheric, and beloved by locals.
Solde Kaffebar
4.6 ★ (771 reviews)
Regementsgatan 2 — between Malmöhus and Turning Torso
Malmö's original specialty coffee bar since 2006. Roasts their own beans on site and supplies other cafés across Sweden. Great sandwiches, croissants, and a genuine neighbourhood feel.
📝 Practical notes: Malmö day trip
Currency: Swedish Krona (SEK), not Danish Krone — but cards accepted everywhere. Train: buy Øresund ticket at Copenhagen Central (not covered by Copenhagen Card). Passport: bring ID just in case (EU/Schengen border). Last trains back run late.
G
Kronborg Castle 🚂
45 min train to Helsingør · Kronborg → Casemates → Harbour Town · 5–6 hours
 ▼
Day trip · A half-day trip north to Helsingør and the UNESCO-listed Kronborg Castle — Shakespeare's Hamlet castle. The train runs direct from Copenhagen Central (45 min, covered by Copenhagen Card). Kronborg is genuinely impressive: a massive Renaissance fortress jutting into the Øresund strait with Sweden visible across the water. The underground casemates are atmospheric and slightly spooky. The harbour town of Helsingør is charming enough for a wander and lunch. You'll be back in Copenhagen by late afternoon.
1
Kronborg Castle
Arrive late morning · 90–120 min
UNESCO World Heritage site and the setting for Shakespeare's Hamlet. A massive Renaissance fortress built in the 1420s, guarding the narrowest point of the Øresund strait. Stunning ballroom, royal apartments, and cannon-lined ramparts with views to Sweden.
Copenhagen Card covers both the train AND castle entry — just scan and go
Look for the Holger Danske statue in the casemates — legend says he'll wake to defend Denmark
2
Casemates (Underground Tunnels)
Included with castle visit · 20–30 min
Dark, atmospheric underground tunnels beneath the castle — originally built for soldiers to shelter during sieges. Dimly lit with candles, slightly damp, and genuinely eerie. The statue of the mythical warrior Holger Danske sits in the deepest chamber.
Bring a phone torch — it's dark down there and the atmosphere is brilliant
Isaac will love the spooky tunnels. Even Lucie might admit they are cool.
3
Helsingør Harbour Town
After the castle · 60–90 min
Charming medieval harbour town with cobblestoned streets, colourful timber-framed houses, and waterfront restaurants. The harbour itself has views to Helsingborg in Sweden (you can take a 20-min ferry across if you fancy). Good for lunch and a relaxed wander.
Stengade is the main pedestrian street — good for a wander and an ice cream
The ferry to Helsingborg, Sweden takes 20 min if you want a bonus country visit
☕ Coffee stops nearby

Kronborg 12A — right at the castle garrison buildings
North Zealand's first micro-roastery, housed in the historic garrison buildings of Kronborg Castle itself. Freshly roasted organic coffee in a uniquely atmospheric setting. A must-stop before or after the castle.
Lagoni Kaffebar
4.6 ★ (Tripadvisor)
Svingelport 3, Simon Spies Plads — central Helsingør
Cosy independent café in the heart of Helsingør. Locals say it serves probably the best coffee in town — excellent cappuccinos with balanced bitterness and perfect foam. Great cakes too.
🚂 Back in Copenhagen by late afternoon
Trains back to Copenhagen run every 20 min. You'll be back by 4–5pm with time for a relaxed evening in the city. Consider dinner at Nyhavn or Torvehallerne if you haven't been yet.
H
Frederiksborg Castle 🚂
45 min train to Hillerød · Castle → Museum → Baroque Gardens · 4–5 hours
 ▼
Day trip · A half-day trip to Denmark's most spectacular castle — Frederiksborg, a Renaissance masterpiece built across three lake islands in Hillerød. The train runs direct from Copenhagen Central (45 min, S-train line A/E to Hillerød, covered by Copenhagen Card). The castle houses the Museum of National History with lavish rooms and a stunning chapel. The baroque gardens behind are beautiful in summer. This is a more relaxed day trip than Kronborg — fewer stops but more time to soak in the grandeur. You could combine this with Day G (Kronborg) for an ambitious double castle day, but each deserves its own visit.
1
Frederiksborg Castle
Arrive late morning · 90–120 min
Scandinavia's largest Renaissance castle, built across three islands on a lake. Constructed by Christian IV in the early 1600s, it's jaw-droppingly grand — red brick, copper spires, and an ornate inner courtyard. The approach across the lake is one of Denmark's most photogenic views.
Copenhagen Card covers the S-train AND castle entry
The Neptune Fountain in the courtyard is a reproduction — the original was stolen by the Swedes in 1659
2
Museum of National History
Inside the castle · 60–90 min
The castle interior houses Denmark's Museum of National History — 500 years of Danish history through portraits, tapestries, and lavishly decorated rooms. The Great Hall and the Chapel (still used for royal ceremonies) are the highlights.
The Chapel is the highlight — it survived a devastating fire in 1859 and retains its original 1620 interior
The Great Hall is one of the largest Renaissance halls in Northern Europe
3
Baroque Gardens
After the museum · 30–45 min
Behind the castle, meticulously restored baroque gardens cascade down a hillside in geometric patterns of hedges, fountains, and flower beds. Free to enter. From the top of the garden you get a beautiful view back across the lake to the castle.
Walk to the top of the gardens for the best photo of the castle reflected in the lake
The gardens are free — you can visit them even if you skip the castle museum
☕ Coffee stops nearby
Café Havehuset
4.0 ★ (Tripadvisor, #7 in Hillerød)
Frederiksborg Castle Gardens — in the park
Set on top of a hill in the castle gardens with a terrace overlooking the grounds. Coffee, cake, and Danish open sandwiches. A beautiful setting — swans on the pond, birdsong, and views of the historic park.
Café Valentin
Local favourite
Central Hillerød — near the station
Popular local café near the station, recommended for lunch before or after the castle. Outdoor patio with castle views. Good coffee and classic Danish lunch dishes. A relaxed spot to refuel.
🚂 Back in Copenhagen by mid-afternoon
S-trains back to Copenhagen run frequently. This is a lighter half-day trip — you'll likely be back by 3–4pm with plenty of evening time. Could combine with a Kronborg (Day G) early morning for an ambitious double castle day.
